NEWSLETTER

第六、七、八特别刊之一

申请贴士  
面试全攻略

一．       可能问到的问题
摘自网上某个地方，以下是最常见的分类列表。如果这些问题都准备好了，那面试一定手到擒来啊。不过我还是坚持要说，面试的最高境界就是无准备，轻松上阵。关键就是放松。

Bring a copy of your transcript and activities resume to your interview. If there is something on your transcript that might need explaining, be prepared.
Questions About Your High School Experience
The questions an interviewer will ask about high school touch topics about which you are most familiar, including your academic background, thoughts about high school, extracurricular activities, and community.

Your Academic Background and School
&#8226;       Tell me something about your courses.
&#8226;       What courses have you enjoyed the most?
&#8226;       What courses have been most difficult for you?
&#8226;       What satisfaction have you had from your studies?
&#8226;       Has school been challenging? What course has been most challenging?
&#8226;       How would you describe your school?
&#8226;       What is the range of students at your school? Where do you fit in?
&#8226;       Do you like your teachers? What is your favorite teacher like?
&#8226;       What do you do in your spare time?
&#8226;       How did you spend last summer?
&#8226;       If you could change one thing about your high school, what would it be?

Your Extracurricular Activities
&#8226;       What extracurricular activity has been most satisfying to you?
&#8226;       What is the most significant contribution you have made to your school?
&#8226;       How would others describe your role in the school community?
&#8226;       What activities do you enjoy most outside the daily routine of school?
&#8226;       Do you have any hobbies or special interests?
&#8226;       Have you been a volunteer?
&#8226;       Would you make different choices of activities if you were to do it all over again?
&#8226;       What do you most enjoy doing for fun? For relaxation? For stimulation?
&#8226;       How do you spend a typical day after school?

Your Community
&#8226;       How would you describe your hometown?
&#8226;       What has been a controversial issue in your community?
&#8226;       What is your position on it?
&#8226;       How has living in your community affected your outlook?

Questions About College
The basic question-one that you should have given significant thought when you did your self-evaluation-is why you want to go to college. There are variations on this theme that may come up during the interview, covering topics such as:
&#8226;       Have you worked up to your potential?
&#8226;       Is your record an accurate gauge of your abilities and potential?
&#8226;       Is there any outside circumstance that interfered with your academic performance? Tell me about it.
&#8226;       What is of the most interest to you about our school?
&#8226;       What other colleges are you considering?
&#8226;       What do you expect to be doing five years from now? Ten years?
&#8226;       Have you ever thought of not going to college? What would you do?

You and the World Around You
This category of questions requires some soul-searching and often includes questions about books you have read, a variation on the hero theme, more probing personal queries, and current events topics. The more selective the college, the more searching these questions tend to be. Be sure you respond with your own ideas and enthusiasms. Don&#39;t quote passages from Thomas Hobbes&#39; Leviathan if you are not prepared to discuss your own views on the philosophical nature of government. In short, don&#39;t paint yourself into the proverbial corner by talking about a topic you are not informed about

What About Your Questions?
Usually toward the end of the interview-sometimes even at the beginning-the interviewer will ask if you have any questions. Be prepared with a list of questions that are specific to each school and try not to ask questions whose answers you could have read in the catalogs or brochures, or on the school&#39;s website. Questions such as the following will prompt engaging conversation:
&#8226;       How does the school treat AP scores? Is there a limit on the number of AP credits the school grants?
&#8226;       What is the system for matching roommates?  
&#8226;       How does advisement work? What is the college&#39;s system for course selection?
&#8226;       Ask about your major. What new offerings are being considered?
&#8226;       Are any new buildings planned? How will that impact the present look of the campus?
If the interviewer is an alumnus, ask him why he chose his alma mater over other s
chools.

二。       顺利面试的建议
What Should I Wear?
For women, nice slacks and a blouse-possibly worn with a blazer-or a simple dress are recommended. Shoes should be low to medium heels. Men should wear nice pants with a shirt, jacket, and tie. A turtleneck and sweater, or shirt and sweater are also possibilities. Jeans and caps are not appropriate. Although you do not want to give a false impression, this is not the time to showcase your purple hair, autographed Phish T-shirt, or two-foot-wide bell-bottoms.
Managing Your Body Language
Everyone who has ever interviewed professionally will tell you that body language can tell a great deal about a person. Body language encompasses the physical cues that communicate information nonverbally, and interviewers are trained to pick up these signals. How can you send the right message with your body language?
&#8226;       Sit still and look directly at the interviewer. If there are multiple interviewers, look at the one who is asking the question at the time.
&#8226;       Holding eye contact for more than five seconds at a time may be considered by some as intrusive. Others may interpret a person&#39;s lack of eye contact as a sign of being shifty or not trustworthy. Try four to five seconds of eye contact, then three to five seconds of looking slightly away, and then back again to making eye contact.
&#8226;       Do not tap your fingers on the table or the chair arm.
&#8226;       Do not tap your feet or swing your legs. Sit up straight in your chair with both feet on the floor.
&#8226;       Breathing slowly and taking time to pause between your thoughts will help you stay calm.
The most important rule, however, is to be yourself and relax.
Personal Pointers
&#8226;       Know the time and location of the interview. Plan to be at least ten minutes early.
&#8226;       Speak positively about yourself without bragging.
&#8226;       Be cheerful and friendly.
&#8226;       Remember that this is a professional setting, so using colloquial language is inappropriate.
&#8226;       Listen carefully to the questions you are being asked and answer them fully.
&#8226;       Be wary of sensitive issues, such as religious or political affiliation. The interviewer may not agree with your viewpoints, so be respectful of his.
&#8226;       Use common sense. Don&#39;t talk about how much you enjoy partying, dislike your physics teacher, or look forward to converting your future classmates to Druidism.
Saying &#39;&#39;Thank You&#39;&#39;
Will sending a thank-you note make the difference between acceptance and rejection? Possibly, but that is not why you are writing the note.


三．       举例—一些问题回答技巧

要学会自己引出话题。可以有意识的在讲一个有意思的事情快结束的时候过渡到另外一个。他自然会问下去，要自己掌握主动权 -- 但绝对是不让对方感觉到的主动权。

过去先问：要不要看看我带的资料？
答案：1。如果他说不要，一般一者说明他已经有了材料了（一些学校会事先给面试官资料了）。或者他会根据你现场和他的谈话来问你问题。这时候说话就要把这点放在心上。
2。如果他说要，那然后一般会根据你的材料来问你。这就可以用你事先准备的一些套路回答了。

（面试官）问：为什么对（政治，文学等你的爱好）感兴趣？
答：
1．       可以提到自己做的project或者research。比如途中遇到困难，怎么解决，然后学到什么。（这种克服困难可以谈很多，可以设计你性格中的坚强，刻苦，独立，钻研，等等，看你自由发挥拉）
2．       即使没有成功的research，只有中途失败的，也可以讲讲为什么失败，从失败中学到什么很有用的东西。这种东西怎么在日后帮助你在别的方面成功。
3．       可以以关怀社会的大方向去讲，适合人文的科目。
4．       希望做出更专业的研究，带动学科进步等方面

如果提到一个你们一同去过或者呆过的地方，一定要抓住机会，通过察言观色，找出共鸣点。我碰到princeton的面试官就是andover毕业的，可是他认识的老师我不认识，我认识的他不认识，惨阿。。。

对学校的了解
这个对方很少故意问，但是对话中可以提到
要了解学校的建校人，建校时间，现任校长，有名的轶事，说话的时候可以不经意的带过，见缝插针，但不要插的太离谱，真的不能插还是不要勉强。。。

为什么要出国和中国教育的评价
对中国教育有所不满很正常，正因为这样你才出国的，但是不要轻易的过分批评中国的整个教育体系。你必须认识到在中国国情下，这个教育体系有它存在的道理。过分的刻意批评一方面显出思想的不成熟，一方面如果你真的对自己的国家有感情，是不会轻易在外国人面前用不屑的神态历数国家的不是。可以谈及自己的个性发展以及这种个性发展和中国教育体制不融合的地方。我自己倒是一直觉得我蛮适合国内的体制也不厌烦，所以出国的一个理由是为了体验另一种截然不同的方式学习，给生活一点乐趣，因为自己比较喜欢尝试新鲜事物，呵呵。如果被问到为什么要出国，又想要避免表现延误国内教育，就往这方面去说吧。

讨论自己的兴趣
我想既然是兴趣，任何人都是很有激情的吧。面试谈到兴趣的时候，是最出彩的地方。这一块最最最最最重要的不是你谈什么，而是你怎么谈的，一定要表现出一种激情，手舞足蹈就很受欢迎阿，哈哈。你要让他感受到你那种投入，陶醉，以及对自己兴趣的热情。

文学：中国古典文学，论语，孔子理论，美国人很感兴趣。如果对方不太了解有恰好没有兴趣的话，或者联系美国社会探讨一下东西文化对比，或者直接转移话题好了。
