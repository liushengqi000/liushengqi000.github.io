BALANCE PARALLEL IDEAS

BALANCE PARALLEL IDEAS

1. balance parallel ideas in a series.
2. balance parallel ideas presented as pairs.
Parallel ideas linked with coordinating conjunctions (and, but, or, nor, for, so, yet)
Parallel ideas linked with correlative conjunctions (either…or, neither…nor, not only…but also, both…and, whether…or)
Comparisons linked with than or as
3. repeat function words to clarify parallels.

#Sue and Terry are completing their math homework, Tom is giving Jim directions to his apartment, Steve is catching up on some sleep, and the rest of the students can be seen daydreaming.

REVISION: ....the rest of the students are daydreaming. （省略号表示省略的部分正确不需要改动）

ADD NEEDED WORDS

ADD NEEDED WORDS
1. Add words needed to complete compound structures.
Difference:  my English professor and mentor:   one person
                    my English professor and my mentor: two persons

2.Add the word that if there is any danger of misreading without it.

# Many civilians believe the air force has a vigorous exercise program.

REVISION: Many civilians believe that the air force …
‘That’ is necessary, for the word tells readers to expect a clause, not just the air force, as the direct object of believe.

3.Add words needed to make comparisons logical and complete.
# the graduation rate of our student athletes is higher than the rest of the student population.
Revision: Our student athletes graduate at a higher rate than the rest of the student population.

4.Add the articles a, an, and the where necessary for grammatical competeness.
But notice:  “a computer and printer” will be also O.K.（习惯用语）

UNTANGLE MIXED CONSTRUCTIONS

UNTANGLE MIXED CONSTRUCTIONS
1.untangle the grammatical structure
&#8226;       有些复杂的句子缺主语或其他成分
&#8226;       English does not allow double subjects; nor an object or an adverb to be repeated in an adjective clause.
&#8226;       太复杂，重新组织

#MIXED: In the whole-word method children learn to recognize entire words rather than by the phonics method in which they learn to sound out letters and groups of letters.
REVISION: The whole-word method teaches children to recognize entire words; the phonics method teaches them to sound out letters and groups of letters.

2.straighten out the logical connections
1) Faulty predication

#We decided that Tiffany’s welfare would not be safe living with her mother.
REVISION: we decided that Tiffany would not …

2) Faulty apposition
#accounting/accountant不能互混

3.avoid “is when”, “is where”, and “reason…is because” constructions.
