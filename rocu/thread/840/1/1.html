叶织文翻译的《春水》

　　2003年10月，冰心网站曾经贴出女学生叶织文翻译冰心的诗集《繁星》，陆续得到了各路高手的指教。那时的叶织文是印度联合世界学院（Mahindra United World College of India）大学预科的学生，2004年，她以优异的成绩被美国包括威尔斯利女子大学等4所大学同时录取。在挑选大学的时候，叶织文将威尔斯利女子大学作为首选，因为那是她所崇拜的冰心奶奶在美留学的母校。现在，她已是一名威尔斯利女子大学的学生了，她十分喜爱这个学校。叶织文同学在认真读书的同时，又翻译了冰心的另一部诗集《春水》。现在将翻译的文稿贴在网站上，希望继续得到行家的指教。

叶织文翻译的《春水》01-60节
叶织文翻译的《春水》61-120节
叶织文翻译的《春水》121-182节

The Author&#39;s Preface


Mother,

Could you take a look

At my miscellaneous writings?

These words

Were already kept in your bosom,

Even before I was born.



--120, “Infinite Stars&quot;

Bingxin

Nov 21, 1922




Spring Water





1



Water in spring,

It is another year,

And you are still running after the breeze.

May I have a look at

My reflection again?



Water replies gently with thanks:

&quot;My friend,

I have never kept a reflection,

Not even yours.&quot;





2



The four seasons slowly pass by-

Hundreds of flowers whisper to each other:

“We are the small and weak!

So, let our lives

Be full of dreams

And our drinking cups

Sentimental,

For God has already arranged all these!&quot;





3



Young People!

You should be

As still and sober as mountains, if

You can’t float with winds,

The flowing wind-like career

Only belongs to the lives of poets.





4



Do reeds

Only accompany the sandy waves?

Please follow up the winds, and travel to Jiangnan*!



*Jiangnan is the area to the south of Yangtze River.





5



A river

Flows with no barrier,

Across ten thousand lis* of sand,

Freely

And silently,

Without a happy voice.



Another river

Bends while it flows,

Across high mountains and deep gorges,

Dangerously

And arduously,

Neither with a happy voice.



My friends!

Thank you for explaining

The questions

I can’t understand for so long.

The happiness of young people

Is rippling with

Both straight and winding streams.



*A &quot;li&quot; is a Chinese unit of measurement.





6



Poets!

Do not grieve nature.

The picture of &quot;beauty&quot;

Needs to be painted lightly.





7



Step by step,

I walked with the support of my hands.

How high and faraway

Is the vague dark-green peak!?





8



The moon,

What makes your dignity?

For in the deep and distant sky,

You come and go alone.





9



If I could reach,

My God!

Where would the limit of your spirit be?

Would you allow me to know?

Might it be so far away

And further

That I felt negligible?





10



I suddenly realize that

It is in the middle of the night.

I won’t let the mood of daytime,

Invade the atmosphere.





11



The south wind blows

Bringing the smile of spring,

From the territory of water.





12



When the music of the xian* approaches,

Listeners come.

When the music of the xian departs,

Will the fate of the ignorant people

Follow?



*Xian is an ancient Chinese stringed musical instrument.





13



White lotus!

Purity confines you.

Why not invite the red lotus in the water

To come and appreciate your elegance?





14



Nature calls out:

&quot;Steep your nib

In my ocean!

The hearts of humans are drying.&quot;





15



Silence

Is

Filled with the songs of triumph.





16



My heart!

When do you start to be vexed?

When

Thinking about the universe

And all living beings.





17



The sun

Over the red walls and feeble grass

Please set soon.

You make many young days wane.





18



The plum flowers in the snow!

You arrive early in the spring.

The little flowers follow you,

Coming into blossom and

Spreading over the ground.





19



Poets!

Please write cautiously!

All bored living beings

Needs your comfort.





20



Standing alone on the peak,

I wondered

Whether I

Possess the whole world.





21



I can only carry my kettle,

Watching my flower wither,

How can the water of sympathy reach her,

My flower,

That I keep in the fence?





22



The pioneer!

Should you open up the path for human beings?

Tighten the string of your heart!





23



The plain pond water,

When facing the sunset,

Becomes a golden ocean.





24



Little island,

How straight and upright you are!

The countless peaks around

Have sunk beneath the sea.





25



The wind in winter is also gentle,

When letting

The flower-like snowflake

Come to full bloom.





26



I am only the weak,

So let me carry

The cross of brightness on my back,

For I want to be rid of

The dim stars

In my nature.





27



While great wind is gathering,

The noise of autumn insects vanishes!





28



The shadow is deceiving the others.

In space,

The moon never turns full and crescent.





29



With the same color of green,

But with a bit more gentleness,

The Lake of Xihu swells,

Like the little sister of ocean.





30



The sky turns bright

As the stars set.

The wind at dawn

Again

Is playing tricks on the sleeping person.





31



Poet!

Nature is commanding you

To calm the tides in your heart

And listen to her call.





32



The fishermen&#39;s boats are coming back.

Look at

The glowing red lanterns

Spreading on the river.





33



Solitary flower in the corner,

As you indulge in self-admiration,

The world becomes smaller.





34



Young people!

From the barren land,

Please search out sympathy!





35



Isn’t the fresh green leaves

The mood for poetry?

For her color turns deeper over time.





36



The &quot;past&quot; of the old

And the &quot;future&quot; of the young,

Are the same

In my deep thinking.





37



The universe!

Uncover your net of stars, and

Allow me to look at your bright face

With reverence.





38



It is late autumn!

The trees have worn their red blouses!





39



Water flows to the East

And the moon sets to the West-

Poet,

Will your heart pull them back?





40



From dusk to late night,

Wild winds sweep through the Huaihua flowers

And sweet rains splash the Tengluo trees.

Can I stop you?

For my sick little brother,

Has just fallen into deep sleep.





41



My little pine tree,

Please allow me to stay with you

As the white clouds on the hill

Are deepening.





42



The lonely sailing boat

Beside the sunset glow,

Unconsciously

Completes the picture of &quot;nature&quot;.





43



Has spring ever spoken up?

But her great potential energy

Makes the world soft.





44



Hold up the flag,

Clever pioneers!





45



Mountains will fall someday,

And oceans will surge.

But the ambition of a common person

Will stand forever.





46



Reserved behavior

Results in liberation of spirits!





47



I stayed in the corridor,

And my book rested on my lap.

With the wind stroking my face,

I knew spring had come.





48



The firefly went away freely,

Leaving the withered lotus alone!





49



In the smile of nature,

The discontent of humans

Will dissolve.





50



Why need to compose?

The poet herself

Is poetry!





51



The crowing of the rooster

Encourages the others,

But rooster himself never needs to be consoled.





52



In the slightly tiring

Deep thought,

The pigeon whistles

Carried on the wind,

Pierce the air for poems.





53



The wind in spring

Sweeps past the fresh grass

With her words to youth:

&quot;My warmness will enlighten you

In the cold,

To create a personality

Of your own.&quot;





54



How does daytime become longer?

The tree shadow by the wall far away

Is too sleepy to move.





55



The lily in the wild land,

Only nature

Will be your friend.





56



In a violent storm--

With the fuzzy distant tree,

The Creator had outlined his picture of dusk.





57



Little spider,

Please stop working,

Your net only gathers dust.





58



Ice is as quiet as a mountain,

But a mountain is as vivid as flowing water.

How can the poet

Manipulate them like this?





59



A passenger is shouting:

&quot;Steersman, be careful

Of the reef in the fog.&quot;



The steersman smiles calmly:

I know the waterway that

I ought to follow,

And that is enough! &quot;





60



Meteors

Are only brilliant

To human beings.

They sweep into darkness

And then tear away.

Is life as uncertain

As meteors?

61



My little brother!

Happy to see you again!

How can the image of you

In my recollection

Be as clear as now.





62



I want to preserve the years of the &quot;past&quot;,

But &quot;now&quot; is weaving her silk threads

Into the net of time.





63



When willow-catkins fly

Swallows come;

When reed-catkins fly,

Swallows go.

But both catkins are equally white.





64



A baby,

In his quivering cry,

He has his mysterious words,

Brought from the brink of life

To address the world.





65



Only a solitary star

In the boundless darkness

Has thoroughly expressed the loneliness of universe.





66



Is clarity

A kind of quiet or aloofness?

Only the firmly standing town wall

And the snow covered willow,

Not horrified by the chill,

Come into the picture

In the vast expanse of white.





67



Belief

Helps the young

Ascend the tower of &quot;submission&quot;

While removing the ladder of &quot;thought”.





68



While I was walking carefully and slowly

On the path extending far into the dark distance,

I listened attentively to my own steps.





69



The silent deep gorge

Sets off the ever-colorful spring blossom.





70



The deep red rose

Was glistening before my eyes.

But when I plucked it

From the branch,

It withered on my garment.



My heart whispers to comfort me:

&quot;You separate the rose from nature,

The deep red goes back to earth;

Young girl,

Be aware of your relentless soul.”





71



When I came back and forth alone

Like the flowing clouds in the sky,

I could feel

The universe is lonely.





72



The gloomy wind in spring

Brings only “troubles”!

The town wall

And the light green willow,

Submerged in the flying dust,

Are sometimes an image

That vexes me.





73



My friends!

If the free spring blossoms

Disturb you by accident,

Please do not blame

For only nature controls them.





74



In the world of uncertainty,

I forget what I said at first, as well as

In the end.





75



I went to the lake yesterday

And listened to the rain today.

The rain drops have splashed into

The pond of my heart.







76



Loneliness augments boredom,

But busyness decreases vexation-

My friend,

Happiness exists in non-stop working!





77



I sit on the steps, talking and laughing-

The setting sun shines on

The pavilion of the hill.

I have always wanted to pay a visit there

And enjoy the pleasure

That ever lasts in my life.





78



Have you ever sat alone

On the fishing deck?

The gentle waves

Watch people revel in the awakening spring.





79



I would like to tell the world

Before I leave:

&quot;I have comprehended you completely!&quot;





80



When I see the leaves turn green again,

I am happy as well as sentimental.

Brave green leaves,

Do you remember your heartrending

Farewell in autumn?





81



Alone, I

Passed the flourishing pine forest

And ascended the steps.

In the Hall of Prayer,

And surrounded by solemn dust,

I could only

Bow deeply to show my respect.





82



My friends!

Don&#39;t let the spring breeze deceive you.

The color of the flower is no better

Than its fragrance.





83



By the mountain gateway in the drizzle,

The steps were wet-

There was only me

With the moving light clouds.               

Are we secret lovers in hiding?





84



In the light of my lantern,

I drew my sword out of the scabbard,

Watching attentively and thinking carefully,

I have filled my bosom with pride,

Forgetting

The fiery blood

And glistening tear.





85



My friends!

If you remember

The lake water in spring,

Be aware that previously

It wasn’t mild,

But icy cold.





86



I descended the steps,

Talking and laughing

In the sunset-

By chance,

I looked back at the red walls

And forward at the yellow tiles.

Instantly, I realized what an “old nation” is,

Then, my heart stirred.





87



Young people!

Do you only look back?

But the world is proceeding without a stop!





88



Spring hesitated to come

To the solemn prayer&#39;s platform-

In the boundless cold,

She could deliver only

The slightest warmth

To the grass

Between the steps.





89



The peach flower

Couldn&#39;t help blowing,

And the grass

Couldn&#39;t help turning green!

The fools!

Why do you pray to nature

For the love to console?





90



Clever ones,

In the vast and lonely world,

You merely

Carry the lantern of “self-confidence”

As you march in the dark.





91



I stared at the silent but gorgeous flowers.

Thinking of the future fruit,

I left them on the branches.





92



Stars!

Humans are gazing at you,

Would you

Draw their eyesight

To outer space?





93



A gust of wind had just passed-

The lake water,

Receded,

While the stone foundation

Marched forth --

What an illusion

In my—my head!!





94



What is the happiness of sowers?

The green traces all over the land

Seen while

Leaning on their hoes.





95



The moon,

While your reflections

In the mirror of water

Brighten here, and

Darken there.

You reside alone in the sky.





96



&quot;When will you come

To observe the moon?&quot;

&quot;Tomorrow.&quot;

But &quot;tomorrow&quot; passed.



&quot;When will you come

And visit the lake?&quot;

&quot;Next year.&quot;

But &quot;next year&quot; passed.



&quot;When will you come

To do some work?

In the next life?&quot;

I was amused

As well as shocked.





97



Where was I

In the midst of dusk?

Mother,

If I could come back to you,

I would cast aside

All the landscape

Outside my heart.





98



I can&#39;t play qin*,

So I listen silently;

I can&#39;t paint,

So I watch thoughtfully;

I can&#39;t express my love for all,

So I pray devoutly.



*The qin is an ancient Chinese musical instrument.





99



&quot;The orchid,

Aren&#39;t you too lonely, and

Don&#39;t you want a friend?&quot;



“I am searching,

But no others

Want to blossom in the empty gorge.





100



When a young man unexpectedly

Gets rid of the burden on his shoulder

His brave heart

Will be sad for his loneliness.





101



My friend!

The final sadness

You should never bear.

On the day

When the Earth collapses,

The Goddess of Happiness

Will smile

To the desperate beings.





102



About my question-

My spirit

Keeps silent

In brightness.

But my dream

Explains in darkness.





103



Daughter of wisdom,

You can never understand

Human sympathy

In the ceaseless resistance.





104



A fish is emerging, while

The bug still remains on the surface

In the situation that

Determines life and death.

My heart quivers.





105



Creator-

If in my long life,

I could only have one wish to the greatest degree,

I would pray sincerely:

&quot;Could I lean on my mother&#39;s bosom,

Could my mother sit in the boat,

And could the boat be

On the sea and under the moon?&quot;





106



From the poet&#39;s heart

Drops

The blood of joy and grief.

The drops

Unconsciously bloom the flowers of sympathy.





107



There are only words,

Arranged across the paper, --

Only words,

Not even phrases or sentences.

In these overlapping jottings

I have left traces of original thoughts.





108



Mother!

The wet nurse should not fool me.

Who was the first

To open the door of sadness in my heart?



-- Please

Wipe the tears in my smile--



The sigh of the homeless lady outside

Broke deeply my heart in pieces

In my dream!

She took my mother away

Then mother is never again

Beside my cradle.

For the first time

I felt the emptiness

Of the world!





109



The long night!

I would like to see some rain.

Then the rain drops as expected-

Let me count

The drops by

The sound of their splashing.

Am I still

Bored?





110



Bright ones!

The graceful and delicate moon

Will be full for some time,

So for the moment,

Just let the light clouds

Cover her.





111



The little sparrow

stops and rests

On the farm.

The croppers on the land

Are waiting quietly for her.





112



The more

Violent

The wave,

The happier

The rock

Standing and guarding

In silence.





113



The little stars

Only whiten a young man&#39;s hair

But can never darken his heart.





114



My friends!

Do not follow me.

The lantern of my heart

Illuminates only my own path!





115



After two rows of candles kindle--

She finds

A little girl in light-red coat hiding

Behind the flowers in the yard.

She would like to recollect

The happiness of her childhood.





116



The window

On the hill has disappeared.

The glow of my lantern

Has died out too!   



In the wind,

The high rock

Stands alone,

Being the helpmate of the grass.





117



The dream

Still does not end-

In the lazy sun

Outside the window,

I met her again!

--Morning glory,

Do you bear

The sorrow

Of last night’s disaster?





118



Ziteng blossom drops

On the pond.

Under the flower pergola,

Not a soul was the witness   

Even in the daytime,

But only the breeze blows its leaves.





119



Is the heart of the poet

Always fit to beat?

The frequent and rapid trembles

Make it weak.





120



&quot;Wish you long life, grandfather!

Let&#39;s drink a toast.&quot;

In the bright light

And in the laughter

His cheeks flush.



Sisters!

Why did God arrange

Our lives like this?

Now the banquet ends

And friends separate-

The night with solitary rain and lonely lamps

Left me only a miserable memory.

121



My people!

Short-lived flowers

Do not deserve

A precious vase.

Yet,

Please treasure

Their brittle lives.





122



The words of nature

Are too profound.

How easy and simple

Are the minds

Of those smart people!





123



The light rains these days

Stopped the messages of spring.

In boredom,

The withered flowers

I picked,

Made me think:

If they are the word’s of last year, then

How can they be the composure for now?



124



It is already dusk-

The waves in the pond

Want to sleep-

But the corridor doesn’t end!





125



The flowers of cultivation

Have withered in silence,

Soon fruit of success

Will emerge in brightness.





126



Rainbow!

Are you regretful?

You appear

Occasionally

In the sky after the rain,

While men and women

On the earth,

Have already painted your figure on their belts.





127



Early dawn

Walks into the garden soundlessly.

While the world is still asleep.

Except for the twinkling dew,

Who else is your companion?





128



The ocean

Breaks my mind

The baby under the cross,

Separated by the clear waves,

Can you just smile superficially?





129



Under the morning sun,

Birds sing melodiously.

Winds sway the drape,

And rub the leaves.

The poet&#39;s heart

Can’t help composing.





130



Now

Who was on the sea boat?

By the waves in the dusk

I leaned against the rail

On the mountain,

Letting my imaginations run away

With me.





131



Young people!

The misery after disappointment

Will bury you

Since we are only modest human beings!





132



The flower again

Is in the vase,

And my book again

In my hand,

But, it is

The night of autumn rain

Of another year.





133



The two yulan blossoms

Blowing last night

On the front of my garment

Learned about

The dusky wind and the morning sun

By heart.





134



Like the sea breeze,

Fate blows on the ferry of youth.

And the ferry

Swings and wanders in the sea of Time.





135



I picked the flower of heaven

In my dream,

But it disappeared

When I woke up-

My friends!

Some wishes in our lives

Reside only in imagination.   





136



The little flower

Growing the cave

Blooms feebly, and

Withers.

She should have experienced the world

Even if she hasn’t appreciate

The spring sunlight.





137



Be silent!

In this infinite world,

I, the insignificant one,

Should only smile

But never speak out.





138



The swinging shadows

Of people

And the low light

Of candles-

Engrave

The mourning of farewell

And the labyrinth of my life

On my first memory.





139



The surging waves of my heart

Are blocked by Leng Yan*.

How can a weak person to understand

Realize the truth of emptiness?



*Leng Yan is Buddhist Classics.





140



Stroll in your dream,

Where there is

Free speech, laughter

And a sincere heart.





141



After the rain

With the croaking of frogs,

The dew on the lotus leaves

Dampens my clothes.





142



The rose has bloomed.

Because of

The boring breeze,

I want never to go to

The little pond again.

Is the poet’s career

Ends with solitude?





143



Please draw aside nature&#39;s curtain!

The Infant of art

Is lying on the bosom of Mother Truth.





144



The poet is writing barely for nothing!

Can her humble heart

Ever console

The painful traveler

In the rain?





145



My heart starts quivering-

When I open the window

In silence,

And face the ocean,

Nature tacitly

Thanks me:

&quot;I admit to be loved.&quot;





146



The flower of experience

Brings about the fruit of wisdom.

But the fruit of wisdom contains

The core of vexation.





147



In the shade of trees,

I am meditating:

The wet spring rays

Dig up the facile hair-like

Air for poetry.

But the gardener

Under the leaves

Cuts it through

In the sound of his scissors.





148



Thank you,

My friends.

Please help yourself

And wear

This white-heart yulan flower.

How can I refuse her?

Even though I never wore

Rose and fragrant orchid

On my hair.





149



My God!

No matter

The gloomy sky, and

Our solitary people,

If there is a soul  

Guarding and accompanying

This dreary and quiescent night,

Sorrow and loneliness

Will vanish from the world.





150



Under the rock

Slowly, the river goes,

And softly the tree shadows

Nod and talk.

And the moon casts her light on

My much feeling for poetry.





151



Who was paddling

On the billows?

The sound of blade and water

Has invaded my deepest thought.





152



Pioneer!

Have you ever stood on the mountain top

And looked into distance?

But please think about

All the suffering living beings

Down at its base

Even if you have

Extricated from agony.





153



Wearing a bamboo hat,

Riding a buffalo,

And thinking with his brow twisted,

A little cowboy, he

Bathes in

The wonderful and soundless praise of

The spring scenery.

How can the poet be happier

Than you?





154



Cut wickers into blades,

And make boats from lotus roots.

Let the little souls in the universe

Flow gently on the ocean of spring.     





155



When the tree recovers from

His sickness.

His shade is even thicker, and

Some small fruit have come to

The flowering branches-

The tree has changed his appearance!!





156



When I woke up-

In the dusk around the corridor,

My thin sleeves fly with the wind;

The courtyard was empty as clear water,

And my heart light as a mirror;

Is the atmosphere for painting or for poem?





157



My sister!

You should enjoy

The good fortune of quiet

By yourself.

Why do you still

Send me these new style poems?

The ponds and hills

That I have never seen,

Come often

To my dreams

And add to the existing bustle.





158



The pioneer!

You should determine the future.

Do not turn back!

Once you relent-

Cowardice hiding in your spirit

Will make you stop.





159



The cool wind was rising gradually

When long I leaned against the bridge.

Where is the home of heaven?

I want to go there with the wind.

Look-

The cold moon

Has become a cloud of light

Flying on the sea.               





160



Nature

Watches the hard-working poet smile:

&quot;When you think and write

Can you ever realize

Even a bit of the limitless solemnity?&quot;



The poet                           

Starts to write!

But a little sadness

Will remain forever in her heart!





161



Behind the window,

I raise my cup-

Bid the falling flowers

Farewell!

I should drink

The clear water

As sweet and fragrant wine.





162



Deep in the cliff

And far away on the sea,

I was fishing alone.

The fish! Had

Better not come,

Since I have already hooked

The mood of poetry from the

Dark blue water.                 





163



In the gray dusk-

I left the mountain gate behind

And gazed at the far village ahead.

On a twisted yellow-soiled path,

I walked mindlessly.



In the grand universe-

Birth is a head;

And death follows.

On the twisted road of life,

I couldn&#39;t help walking.



The path to the future is thus long and far!

I raised my head, seeing the crescent moon,

Its unspeakable touch

Aroused my heart.





164



When I will leave soon,

I can see clearly

My boat

Arriving soon

In the midst

Of green mountain and blue river.

The light clouds,

Though pressing on my gloomy heart

Are not able to envelop my dream-like song.





165



My friend,

Sit down and do not wander,

Your moving shadow in the water

Will frighten the fish.





166



Pointing to the farthest peak,

I see a pine tree stands alone.

I wonder

If I can lean against it

And watch the sunset.



Dusk is approaching,

So I must reconsider this trip.

For my clothes are thin, and

The wind is cold.

It is better to return.





167



Beside the green water,

Swim a couple of ducks,

And a few young girls wash clothes.

Nature unrolls a picture

In front of the poet on her donkey.





168



Amidst the dim moon light

In the quiet courtyard,

By the long corridor,

I can even hear the dropping flowers,

As well as the clangs of the rock bell

Breaking the silence of the night.





169



When an unborn baby

Hold the window of “life”

And Observe

From outside of the world,

He would see the hazy cavern of “death”.





170



To mourn the ceaseless noise of artillery,

Ruining the lives in the world,

In the quiet night,

I caught

The moths seeking their dooms

In my hand

And released them

Out of the window.





171



Where the horse clops by,

Dust flies.

Holding the reins, I see

The vast green prairie-

Limitless grief remains,

For how dare the poet dream to

Be a hero?





172



When I opened the letter,

I was sitting under the flowers.

A gust of cool wind

Blew away several pages

That I had just read.

I only stared silently,

Listening to the wind

As it reached the corner of the wall,

My relieved and happy soul

Will rise with the flying paper.     





173



Under the bright moon,

The green leaves look like clouds

And the white clothes like snow-

How touching is the scene

On the night of farewell!!





174



Young people,

Write with care,

For Time turns the pages

Asking you to start.





175



Seeded with doubt, I

Closed my window

And began musing.

When I opened my window

Again with doubt,

Not only had the seed burgeoned,

But traces of green

Had crept into others&#39; gardens.





176



Flowers on the battlefield!

I extol your deepest love!

You blow bravely in the hail of bullets.

And give comfort to the newly dead.





177



My heart saddens suddenly.

Last night, I dreamed that

I alone wore clothes of ice

While traveling across the sea

Of darkness

On the surging crests.





178



I seated myself

On the damp steps.

Green leaves-

When the rose withered and fell,

Both you and the poet

Felt the loneliness.





179



The bright moon,

Your mourning is complete!

In the silvery field,

Who is blowing the pipe

On the other side of the river?





180



Infant!

Whose praise is as fond?

When he murmurs

To the evening glow

At the end of the sky,

My powerless pen

Has to give up work.





181



Took off the flowers

On the front of your garment

In a hurry,

I made them a souvenir of farewell.



The horse had already

Arrived at the door.

If I hadn&#39;t heard her laughter

Through the window,

I would have missed her;

Then when would we reunite?





182



Farewell,

Water in spring!

Thank you for murmuring all this season

And silencing my worry.



I wave you goodbye,

For you will flow to the human world.

I will sit by your source

Listening to your echo.





March, 5-June, 14, 1922
