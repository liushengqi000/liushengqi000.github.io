  Purpose  


This program offers financial aid to international students from families of modest means who require assistance to enable them to attend McGill. Since a limited number of awards are available, financial need is the primary criterion in the selection of award recipients. The bursaries will help to cover a portion of the international tuition fees.



  Eligibility  


This program is open to students admitted to McGill, entering the first year of full time undergraduate studies in the Fall term. In addition, applicants must complete an International Student Financial Aid Profile form and Certification of Finances form and mail it to the International Student Services office.

Note: You may only apply once to this program.



  Application Procedure  


Applicants must complete the following three forms:

International Student Financial Aid Profile  
Certification of Finances  
McGill Entrance Bursary Application for International Students (Link at bottom of page)




  Application Deadline  


Thirty days from the date of your letter of acceptance.



  Notification of Award  


The Student Aid Office will attempt to respond to all applicants within two to three weeks of receipt of the application along with the supporting documents (e.g. financial aid profile, certification of finances). Only original copies will be accepted (no faxes, copies). Decisions will be posted on Minerva and award recipients will be sent written confirmation via courier. This document may constitute a portion of your proof of financial resources required to secure a CAQ and a Study Permit from Immigration authorities.

Note: Applications missing a financial aid profile and/or a certification of finances will not be assessed.



  Other Awards  


Students who do not qualify for the Entrance Bursary program may, once they are in attendance, apply for a program of assistance for in-course students which includes loans, bursaries and a Work Study program. Students may request an interview with a Student Aid Counsellor at any time during the course of the academic year to discuss their financial situation.
