三。钱钱钱：奖学金与申请

三。钱钱钱：奖学金与申请
对很多人来说，高等教育的学费是个很大的拦路虎。所以关于资金的投入和分配要尽早的放入申请日程中。在你申请的一开始，就最好和你的父母开诚布公的讨论确定你们家庭每年能够以及愿意付的学费。 （可以参考www.collegeboard.com上面的一个计算器来对家庭的支出做个大概的了解）。那么你是不是准备奖学金？如果是，便可以开始搜查各种奖学金。

关于大学花费，请注意以下几点：
&#8226;       奖学金是以package的形式发放的，会同时包括奖学金，贷款，在校工作等部分。
&#8226;       Need-blind意思是招生办在决定是否录取你的时候不会考虑你的家庭财政情况。但不表示收入的学生将会得到他所需要的全部钱。
&#8226;       Need-based意思是一个学生一旦入学，学校就会根据他的家庭情况和学校的计算公式，给足奖学金。但是招生办招人的时候，家庭情况可能会纳入考虑范围。
&#8226;       同时是need-based和need-blind的学校少之又少。
&#8226;       非常常见的一个现象是，一个学生从不同的学校那里拿到的奖学金差别非常大。奖学金的组成也差别非常大，有的都是奖学金，有的多数是贷款。所以需要奖学金的同学要慎重考虑是否ed/ea。
&#8226;       下面这个情况不少人也是碰到过的：一个学校录取了你，但是给不够你钱。或者一个学校还没录你，但是暗示你说，如果你肯自己多出点，或者不要钱，就录取你。
&#8226;       美国大学给国际学生的奖学金非常有限。

在选校的时候，很有必要将奖学金作为最重要的一个因素来考虑，要选那些学费低的，或者奖学金慷慨的地方申请。比较便宜的总是那些公立学校。另外，加拿大的学校教育也很出色，学费比美国的要便宜不少。
另外有一些学校为学习特别出色的学生提供奖学金。这部分不是之前提到的need-based的奖学金，而是merit-based. 是给那些有特殊成就的学生。
如果对任何学校的奖学金有不清楚的地方，最好及时联络学校的奖学金办公室主任，尽早搞清楚对方是不是给国际学生提供奖学金。每个学校每年的政策也都在变。

一。选校2

一。选校2
选校是个累人的活，也是个很重要的活。很多人只被一所学校录取但被所有其他学校据，可见选校决定成不成功阿！
开始的时候不要对任何学校先入为主，要客观的评价。在选校的时候，记住 （a）你的实力和这个学校的实力比较匹配，不能互相差的太离谱（b）你有清楚的理由选择这个学校并且在那里度过四年 （c）要有匹配的，也要有水平稍高的，还要有保底的，学校的分配比例要合理。

在选校过程中，你喜欢的学校类型会摇摆不停。这种改变是正常也是很正确的发展方向。这意味着你很认真的思考了这个问题。不少学生一开始只是坚持这要去某一特定类型的学校，去参观之后才发现很失望。国内的同学不能参观，这是一个很大的劣势，所以更需要一开始选校，不要抱有什么偏见，不要固执于排名，不要误以为lac比不上大u，也不要觉得城市的学校比郊区的好，更不要觉得女校不好。

女生绝对应该好好关注一下女子学校，美国的女子学校都是非常非常不错并且很有个性的。男校么。。好像不怎么样。。哈哈。

二。选校中要问的一些问题

二。选校中要问的一些问题

选校最首先就是要根据奖学金发放情况，把那些不给钱的学校都去掉，留下慷慨的，和学费不高的学校，然后就是要选择你喜欢的那一类型了。
在试图了解一个学校大概是什么样子的时候，可以问以下的问题
（请注意，我知道国内的学生相比美国学生会功利不少，也确实是形势所逼，不得不以收人难易和慷慨与否来决定学校，很少有人一条条用下面的问题去研究学校，不过这些问题都可以好好记住，可以在面试的时候问面试官，嘿嘿~~）

Student Environment (campus and student life, housing, types of students)
What are the students like? Do the students look and act like you? Can you see yourself here?
What is the style of the school? The mood on campus?
What kinds of religious, cultural, and special interest opportunities exist here?
How do students get to know one another? What do students do on the weekends? What is the drug and/or alcohol scene?
Is there Greek life (sororities and fraternities)? What percent of the students join? What are social and residential opportunities outside the Greek system?
Where do most freshmen live? How are rooms assigned to freshmen? To upperclassmen?
How competitive are the athletic programs? How difficult is it to make a varsity team? Is there an active intramural program? Are men’s and women’s teams equally supported – in terms of facilities, access to the best practice times, best fields, best trainers, best transportation, etc.

How competitive is the orchestra? Theater? What are th options?
What is the level of student satisfaction? What is the attrition rate? What is the percentage of students that graduates in four years? In six years?

Academic Environment (philosophy, curriculum, faculty)
&#8226;       How would you describe the atmosphere ther – traditional, progressive, scholarly or career-oriented, politically active? What are the most important issues on campus today?
&#8226;       What are the most popular departments? What are perceived to be the strongest?
&#8226;       In your academic areas of interest, how strong are the departments – as measured by the number and types of courses offered, the equipment and facilities available, opportunities for research, the number of faculty? Are you more likely to work with a professor or a graduate student?
&#8226;       How many of your courses will be requirements? How many will be electives? Is there a core curriculum or distribution requirements?
&#8226;       What is the workload like? Do courses tend to emphasize exams, papers, oral presentations? What is the average class size for freshmen and upperclassmen?
&#8226;       Do students have access to faculty? Do all professors teach undergraduates? Do they post office hours? Do they ever eat with students or meet with them informally?
&#8226;       Who is likely to be an advisor? What does an advisor do? How much are you likely to see him/her? How are advisors assigned? Can you change your advisor?
&#8226;       How are you graded? Do students receive many written comments from teachers?
&#8226;       Is theere an honor code? What does it entail? Does it work?
&#8226;       How would you characterize the degree of academic pressure and competition? Is it worse among students pursuing certain majors or preparing for certain careers?
&#8226;       Do most students stay for four years? Do many students pursue special majors, double majors, internships, a junior year abroad, exchanges, work-study? What types of special programs are most popular? Is it difficult to take part in these programs?
&#8226;       What can you learn about the school’s career services office? What kind of help will this office give you in finding vacation and post-graduation employment?
&#8226;       What happens during Freshman Orientation? When does it begin? What is required and what is optional?
&#8226;       Is there a required internship, senior thesis or project? Is there a required comprehensive exam? How many students participate in honors programs?

Physical Environment
&#8226;       How safe are the campus and the surrounding area? What security precautions does the college take?
&#8226;       What are the transportation links between the college and the “outside world”, especially your hometown?

Costs
&#8226;       What is the estimated total cost for tuition, room and board, fees, and other college-related expenditures (i.e. books, travel between home and school, entertainment)?
&#8226;       Outside of books and travel, on what do students spend the most money? About how much money will you need to spend per week? How much are social fees for fraternities and student activity fees?
&#8226;       What are the opportunities for part-time jobs for students who are not ono financial aid?

Financial Aid
&#8226;       Does the college practice a need-blind admission policy?
&#8226;       What percentage of students had their full need met?
&#8226;       Are merit scholarships offered? How are studnets chosen?
&#8226;       Do the same financial aid procedures and policies apply for the entire four years?
&#8226;       Will you receive a similar financial aid package during all four years if your family’s financial situation does not change?
&#8226;       How are outside scholarships handled? Are they credited against the loan component of your aid, the grant component, or the family contribution?
&#8226;       Do you need a certain grade point average to keep your institutional grant? If so, what is it?
&#8226;       Are there any tuition payment plans that will allow you and your parents to spread out your payments over a period of months?
&#8226;       What is the policy with regard to non-custodial parents and stepparents?
&#8226;       Are they expected to contribute if financially able?
&#8226;       How much indebtedness can you expect after four years?
&#8226;       Is home equity considered when financial aid is calculated?
&#8226;       How many hours a week will you have to work to fulfill the work-study portion of your aid package?
