Nets 100, Heat 88
Jefferson Turns Ankle, So Kidd Turns Up Heat
By JOHN ELIGON

MIAMI, May 8 — When Jason Kidd struggled to find the rim in the Nets&#39; first-round playoff series against the Indiana Pacers, the Nets maintained that his scoring was not as crucial to their success as his playmaking.

Indeed, the Nets got by the first round with Kidd averaging 8 points a game and shooting 29 percent from the field.

But the question lingered: What could the Nets do if Kidd found his range?

The answer came Monday night. The Nets could spring so far ahead of the Miami Heat that not even a fourth-quarter hiccup, or an injury to Richard Jefferson, could make the Nets crumble.

Despite a late charge by the Heat, the Nets led by as many as 28 points and cruised to a 100-88 victory in Game 1 of this best-of-seven second-round series. The Nets have beaten the Heat four consecutive times.

Kidd finished with 22 points, 9 rebounds and 7 assists. Vince Carter led the Nets with 27 points. The Nets shot 48.6 percent from the field and held the Heat to 39.1 percent.

&quot;Jason does what the team needs,&quot; Nets Coach Lawrence Frank said. &quot;When he started making those shots on the high pick-and-roll, Vince was out of the game, so he knew he had to pick it up. That&#39;s the great leadership with Jason. It&#39;s not about his scoring. It never will be and it never has been.&quot;

Miami Heat Coach Pat Riley said Kidd&#39;s play was the key to the game. &quot;He was as good as I&#39;ve seen him tonight,&quot; Riley said.

Although Heat center Shaquille O&#39;Neal finished with 20 points and 10 rebounds, the Nets held him to 7 points through three quarters. The Nets also forced O&#39;Neal into foul trouble, limiting him to fewer than 30 minutes.

Dwyane Wade led the Heat with 25 points, but he was held scoreless in the fourth quarter.

The Nets may be forced to move forward with cautious optimism. Jefferson, one of the driving forces behind their hot start, left the game less than two minutes into the third quarter with a sprained right ankle.

Jefferson appeared to roll the ankle as he was driving to the basket. He fell to the floor and clutched it. X-rays showed that nothing was broken.

Jefferson, who scored 20 points, left the arena on crutches, unwilling to commit to his status for Game 2 on Wednesday night.

&quot;Guys, guys, can&#39;t tell you how I&#39;m going to feel in two days,&quot; he told reporters after the game. &quot;I&#39;m very confident that I can deal with quite a bit of pain. I feel that I can deal with more pain than most individuals.&quot;

Jefferson did indicate that he might have been able to return to Monday night&#39;s game had the circumstances been different.

&quot;We were up, the game was under control,&quot; he said. &quot;There was no need to really force it or force the issue.&quot;

Without Jefferson, the Nets nearly let their big lead slip away. The Heat staged an 18-3 run in the fourth quarter to put the Nets on edge and pump life back into American Airlines Arena, which was already clearing out.

O&#39;Neal made a layup with 4 minutes 21 seconds to play that cut the Heat&#39;s deficit to 92-83. It was the first time the Nets&#39; lead had been in single digits since 5:53 remained in the first quarter. But the Heat got no closer.

&quot;Anytime you dig a hole like that, it&#39;s highly unlikely you&#39;re going to come back and win,&quot; Heat forward Udonis Haslem said. &quot;It was frustrating.&quot;

The Nets came out with an offensive onslaught the likes of which they had yet to show in this postseason. They shot 71.4 percent from the field in the first quarter and scored 38 points.

Kidd perhaps best epitomized the Nets&#39; first-half offense. His 3-pointer with 3:23 to go in the first half increased his point total to 17, which was more than he scored in any game in the first round against the Pacers.

The reserves Lamond Murray and John Thomas also made important contributions. Murray hit a pair of timely 3-pointers and finished with 8 points. Thomas, whom the Nets signed with two games remaining in the regular season, played 16 arduous minutes of defense against O&#39;Neal, covering for the foul trouble of power forwards Jason Collins and Cliff Robinson.

Collins was also forced out of the game because he reopened a cut under his chin when he collided with Wade in the first quarter. But before leaving the game, Collins did his part in frustrating O&#39;Neal into foul trouble.

O&#39;Neal went to the bench with 7:03 remaining in the first quarter after picking up his second offensive foul with Collins guarding him. He had to leave the game again at the 8:35 mark of the third quarter after picking up his fourth foul.

O&#39;Neal did not speak to reporters after the game. It was perhaps the loudest statement of the Heat&#39;s frustration.
