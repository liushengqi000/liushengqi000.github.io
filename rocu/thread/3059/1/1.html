ED: Early Decision, blinding. If you are a freshman applicant who is certain that One college is your first-choice college, and you are prepared to attend if you are offered admission, you are encouraged to apply under the Early Decision Option. Students applying as Early Decision candidates will receive their admission decision in December or early January. If you are offered admission through Early Decision, you must withdraw all applications you may have submitted to other colleges and pay certain tuition deposit within three weeks of notification. You can only apply to one college under ED.

EA: Early Action, non-blinding. Similar to ED, Only superiorly renowned colleges use such policy, such as Harvard and Yale. And you will be offered admission results in mid-December, however, just need to reply before May, 1st. You can only apply to one college under EA.

RD: Regular Decision, non-blinding, you can apply to as many colleges as you wish under RD and will be notified results in early April. You also will be asked to reply on May,1st and pay the correspondent deposit.

Rolling is circular matriculation, no comments out of no experiences.
