ArticleⅡ. Purpose
ROCU is concerned with the support and enhancement of extracurricular activities in China by appropriate means such as publications, volunteer programs, workshops, and other educational and practical activities.
The purpose of ROCU shall be to promote great awareness and understanding of public service in communities, develop the skills of leadership among its members, and foster the values of diligence, teamwork and service.

Article Ⅲ. Membership

Section 1. Membership in ROCU shall be open to all students who is studying or has studied in Chinese schools, or whoever is willing to learn more about Chinese culture and contribute to public service in China, regardless of race, creed, color, sex, sexual orientation, gender orientation, or physical disability.

Section 2. Active Members are those who take part in ROCU’s projects at least once a year and register officially at the official website: www.rocu.cn.

Section 3. Membership will be retained until the Student severs relations with ROCU. Any member may resign by filing a written resignation with the Administrative Committee.

Section 2 Committees
PCI shall consist of the following committees for the purpose of division of responsibilities:
Clause 1. The Administrative Committee, headed by the Administrative Chair, shall coordinate all logistical and administrative needs of PCI, recruit new members and officers, and keep records of all meetings and projects, including, but not limited to agendas, attendance lists, membership lists, and minutes. By the end of the term, the committee shall archive all above documents.

Clause 2. The Fundraising Committee, headed by the Treasurer, shall maintain the group finances, including preparing the overall annual budget and annual financial report(s), keeping accurate records of financial affairs and presenting a monthly financial report to the board. The committee shall also obtain donations of money or goods to support the programs of PCI and plan all fundraising activities.

Clause 3. The Public Events Committee, headed by the Public Events Chair, shall organize an ongoing series of different programs, coordinate all directors in the Committee taking charge of different projects and directly administer every aspect of the projects.

Clause 4. The Development Committee, headed by the Development Chair, shall plan new projects not covered in ROCU’s projects yet, including estimating the budget, seeking local and overseas support, locating partner schools and in the end, proposing a complete plan to the Board.

Clause 5. The Publicity Committee, headed by the Publicity Chair, shall maintain all aspects of ROCU tech operations, including the website, forum, networks, accounts update and server, publicize and promote the events and efforts to the public, and take charge of publications of PCI.

  PCS shall consist of the following committees:
Clause 6. The Administrative Committee, headed by the Administrative Chair, shall coordinate all logistical and administrative needs of PCS, recruit new members and officers, and keep records of all meetings and projects, including, but not limited to agendas, attendance lists, membership lists, and minutes. By the end of the term, the committee shall archive all above documents.

Clause 7. The Fundraising Committee, headed by the Treasurer, shall maintain the group finances, including preparing the overall annual budget and annual financial report(s), keeping accurate records of financial affairs and presenting a monthly financial report to the board. The committee also shall obtain donations of money or goods to support the programs of PCS and plan all fundraising activities.

Clause 8. The Public Events Committee, headed by the Public Events Chair, shall organize an ongoing series of different programs in cooperation with PCI, providing overseas support.

Clause 9. The Development Committee, headed by the Development Chair, shall seek new opportunities in foreign universities to establish long-term cooperation with Chinese high school counterparts. (After the negotiation and settlement of new projects, the projects shall be taken over by the Public Events Committee.)

Section 3 The Board:
Clause 1. The leadership of ROCU consists of a board of twelve board officers.

Clause 2. All board members shall be equally empowered within ROCU and equally responsible for the welfare of ROCU and the fulfillment of its purpose as stated above.

Clause 3. The Board shall be responsible for planning and overseeing the execution of ROCU’s activities and projects.

Clause 4The Board shall have the authority to create any special committees, subcommittees, or positions.

Clause 5. The Board must approve the Society’s budget and authorize any disbursement of funds.

Clause 6. The Board shall prepare the Annual Report of ROCU’s activities, projects, and financial standing at the conclusion of its term. The report shall be made available to the members of ROCU on the official website.

Section 4 Board Officers
Clause 1. The twelve Board Officers shall consist of: six members from PCI (President, Administrative Chair, Treasurer, Public Events Chair, Development Chair and Publicity Chair); five members from PCS (President, Administrative Chair, Treasurer, Public Events Chair, and Development Chair); a Chief Secretary.

Clause 2. The Presidents shall be the chief executive of PCI and PCS and shall maintain general coordination of its committees and projects. The President is especially responsible for organizing meetings, and planning the overall management of every part of ROCU. By the end of his/her term, the President shall finish the annual report.

Clause 3. The Chief Secretary shall coordinate the management of PCI and PCS, especially the coordination of both. The Secretary shall also arrange meetings of board officers, and keep record of board meetings.

ArticleⅤ. Elections
Section 1
Clause 1. All active members, as defined in Article Ⅲ, Section 2, shall have one vote by secret ballot with the only exception of the Chief Secretary. The Chief Secretary shall not vote at any time.

Clause 2. An impartial Election Commissioner shall be appointed by the Board at least one week in advance of the Election. The Election Commissioner may not run for any possible and may not be a current Board Officer. The Election Commissioner shall ensure the election to be a fair process conducted in an orderly fashion.

Clause 3. The voting shall be held online. All members eligible to vote are responsible to check the online information and vote in time.

Section 2
Clause 1. The term of officer shall start on the first day of September and shall terminate on the last day of August.

Clause 2. Officers shall be elected annually by the members of ROCU. Elections shall be held during the first two weeks of August in a public election. Members of ROCU shall be informed of the exact date of election at least two weeks in advance.

Clause 3. The Officers shall be elected in the following order:
Chief Secretary, Presidents, Committee Chairs, Committee Members
All candidates for a specific Officer position that were not elected may at their discretion run for another Officer position that has not been elected yet.
All candidates can run for at most two committees spontaneously.

Clause 4. Chief Secretary candidate shall be a student enrolled at a university (either in China or overseas), including incoming freshman, and shall be working in the Board or any of the committees for at least one year.

Clause 5. All candidates for Presidents and Committee Chairs shall be working in the Board or any of the committees for at least one year.

Clause 6. Every member who will be staying in China during the following term is eligible to run for any position in the PCI committees.
        Every member who will be staying at an overseas school during the following term is eligible to run for any position in the PCS committees.

Clause 7. Each candidate must fill out an application and have an interview (in person or through the Internet) with an assigned Board Officer or a delegate appointed by the Board before deadline. Deadline of each year shall be informed to every member when application for the coming term is available.

Clause 8. Campaigns shall be held on the forum, and the answers to essay questions on the application will be posted on forum so that every member has access to them.

Clause 9. The candidate with most votes shall become the elected Officer. If a candidate is elected for two or three of the positions he/she runs for, he must choose which position to take office in 24 hours. For the position(s) he/she gives up, the candidate with the second most votes shall become the elected officer.

Clause 10. In case of a tie, a second round of elections shall be held amongst the tied candidates.
