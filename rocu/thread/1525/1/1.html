Funding for Overseas Students

It is a requirement of the University of Cambridge that all overseas (non-EU) students holding offers are to provide a financial guarantee of their ability to pay the university fees, college fees and meet all living costs for themselves and any accompanying dependants throughout the duration of their course of study prior to their arrival in Cambridge. For an Engineering undergraduate student, for example, the total amount involved is around &pound;21k per year, which requires a total financial guarantee of around &pound;84k for the 4 year M.Eng.  The total figure includes all fees and living costs for 9 months of the year, but not travel or living costs in the summer (July-September) when most students return home. It is sometimes possible for overseas students to obtain well-paid work in the UK during vacations. However, the intense nature of the Cambridge course means that students are not normally able to undertake paid work during university terms: in any event before committing yourself to a work commitment of any sort you would need to consult the College that makes you an offer of a place, and Colleges do not normally accept that money earned in this way can be included as part of a financial guarantee.

Most Colleges do not offer financial help to overseas undergraduates. All candidates who hold an offer of a place at any College can, however, apply through their College to the Cambridge Overseas Trust for a part-cost Bursary as a contribution towards the full cost of their undergraduate studies. Unfortunately the Trust has very limited resources and applicants should not depend on support from this source. In any event, candidates should recognize that the Trust is likely to offer only a relatively small contribution towards the full costs of studying in Cambridge and that competition for funding is severe.

At present no funding is available specifically from the Ameson/Cambridge scheme, in particular, and no scholarships are awarded as part of any PRC-based selection process.

All queries should be addressed directly to the Admission’s Office, Fitzwilliam College, admissions@fitz.cam.ac.uk
